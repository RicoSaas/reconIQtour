INSTITUTION MODE
ONCE YOU SEE THE DATA

Not just a red flag.
A numbered playbook.

📋 For every chargeback flag
  • 1Ask your acquirer for a reason code breakdown — most disputes fall into a handful of codes, each with a specific fix.
  • 2Fix your transaction descriptor. "Transaction not recognised" is usually caused by a confusing merchant name on the bank statement.
  • 3Make your refund policy visible and fast. Customers who can't get a refund easily escalate to a chargeback.
  • 4Contact your acquirer proactively — before they contact you. Self-reporting shows good faith.
  • 5Document your remediation plan in writing. Card schemes favour merchants who show a structured response.
🔒 For every late reserve release
  • 1Wait 5 business days after the expected date — acquirers often process releases in batch cycles.
  • 2Email your acquirer's settlements ops team — not your account manager. Reference the statement date, amount, and calculated release date.
  • 3No response in 5 days: escalate in writing, copy compliance or legal. State the hold period has elapsed per contract terms.
  • 4Check your merchant agreement. Holding beyond the contractual period without justification is a breach of contract.
🏦 For institutions — escalation ladder
  • ①Send written warning to merchant (VAMP ≥ 0.65%)
  • ②Issue formal notice with 30-day improvement window (ECM ≥ 0.90%)
  • ③Increase reserve percentage applied to settlements (ECM+)
  • ④Suspend MID (HCM ≥ 1.80%)
  • ⑤MATCH list referral on persistent breach
⚠️ Red Alert Protocol
  • →Screenshot flag with timestamp
  • →Notify MLRO within 2 hours
  • →Contact merchant or acquirer same day
  • →Document every action with timestamp
  • →Discrepancy > £10,000 unresolved after 48h: begin SAR assessment procedure
